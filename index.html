Discover UK

When you only got one year in Manchester

Cotswolds


Declared an Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty the glorious, honey-coloured towns and villages of the Cotswolds look as if they have strayed into the 21st century from another era.

Lake District


The Lake District is by far the UK's most popular national park. Ever since the Romantic poets arrived in the 19th century, its postcard panorama of craggy hilltops, mountain tarns and glittering lakes has been stirring the imaginations of visitors.

Anglesey


For fans of castles and coast this is the place to visit. Beyond the sands and stones, this part of Wales offers rich opportunity for various water sports, walking and birdwatching.

Orkney Islands


This scattered archipelago of 70 islands has a time-worn, slightly mystical charm of its own.On the Orkney Islands, travelers will be rewarded with storm-battered cliffs, ancient stone circles, and ominous-looking “sea stacks,” which rise from the churning blue water like swords.

Cornwall


You can't get further west in England than the ancient Celtic kingdom of Cornwall. Blessed with the southwest's wildest coastline and most breathtakingly beautiful beaches, this proudly independent peninsula has always marched to its own tune.

Peak District


Rolling across the Pennines' southernmost hills is the glorious Peak District National Park. Ancient stone villages are folded into creases in the landscape, and the hillsides are littered with stately homes and rocky outcrops.
